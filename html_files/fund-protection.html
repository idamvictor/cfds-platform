Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

What happens to my funds if 1 Trade Market becomes insolvent?

Your funds are held in legally segregated accounts entirely separate from 1 Trade Market's company assets. Under Swiss law, these cannot be used to satisfy company debts. Additionally, our enhanced insurance policy covers losses above $10,000 up to $1,000,000 per client, meaning your capital is protected even in the worst-case scenario.

How is the fund manager security deposit used to protect me?

Every fund manager must post a mandatory security deposit (between $20M and $500M depending on their AUM) before managing any client capital. This deposit is held in a locked trust account. If a manager engages in misconduct, fraud, or causes losses due to negligence, this deposit is liquidated first to compensate affected investors, before any other recourse is taken.

What qualifications does a fund manager need to be approved?

Managers must complete a 6-stage vetting process: identity and entity verification, a 3-year audited track record, AML/sanctions screening, risk framework evaluation, security deposit lodgement, and ongoing monitoring. We require a minimum Sharpe ratio of 1.2, maximum drawdown of 20%, and a valid regulatory licence in their home jurisdiction.

Can I verify where my funds are held?

Yes. All clients have access to our real-time Transparency Portal, which shows the total balance held in segregated accounts at each custodian institution, reconciled daily by an independent agent. You can log in at any time and verify that your account balance is fully represented in our segregated holdings.

Is my insurance coverage automatic or do I need to register?

Insurance coverage is automatic for all registered clients, there is no separate registration or opt-in required. Coverage applies from the moment your account is verified and active. The policy is maintained by 1 Trade Market at no cost to you, and applies to all account types including standard trading accounts and Retirement Staking™ accounts.

How do I revoke a fund manager's access to my account?

You can revoke a fund manager's access at any time, instantly, from the Trade Access page in your dashboard. The revocation takes effect immediately, the manager loses all access and cannot place any further trades. Any open positions remain yours to manage, and a full audit log of all activity during the managed period is available for download.
